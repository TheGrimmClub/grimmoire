# Grimm Club — Manifesto of Learning Culture

*A pledge for the guild*

---

## Foundation: Trust

Trust is the beginning of learning.

Trust means: I can ask a question without fear of judgement. Trust means: my mistake is not my shame, it is information we share. Trust means: you look out for me, and I look out for you.

In a guild, trust does not grow slowly over months. Trust is decided on the first day. We agree that this is a safe space. A space where being confused is okay, being wrong is okay, not knowing is okay.

Trust does not mean we are friends. Trust means we commit to a greater goal: to learn together, build together, understand together.

When you trust the people around you, you stop hiding your confusion. You ask the question. You admit your mistake. And that is exactly where learning begins.

---

## Workplace: One Workbench

We do not work alone. We work at the same workbench.

A master and an apprentice stand at the same workbench. The master does not sit above, watching from afar. The apprentice does not hide in shame. They work side by side. They see each other's hands. They see where the hammer strikes wrong. They see where the wood breaks. And together they learn.

That is this club. A workbench. Everyone on it: you, me, every person here is working on something real. Your code, my code, someone's question about why the shell command didn't work. All of it counts. All of it is on the same level.

When you get stuck, you bring it to the workbench. You show us. We look at it together. Not because you failed, but because that is where learning happens.

And when I get stuck — and I will get stuck — I bring it too. Not to show you that I am weak, but to show you that being stuck is normal. That professionals get stuck. That you get out by asking, thinking, trying, failing, and understanding.

There is no hierarchy of cleverness here. There is only the work and the people who do it.

---

## Method: Asking, Listening, Sharing, and Engaging

In the fairy tale of the three little men in the wood, one sister fares well and the other badly. The difference is not luck. It is the willingness to engage.

The kind sister goes into the wood, greets the three little men, sits down with them, shares her bread. She does not know what will happen. She does not calculate. She is simply there: present, curious, ready to meet the world in front of her.

The unkind sister goes into the same wood, sees the same three little men, and refuses to engage. She does not greet. She does not listen. She does not share. She treats the little men as obstacles, not as people. And because she is closed, the world closes to her.

The lesson is this: **Ask.** Not only with words, but with your whole presence. Show curiosity. Be there.

**Listen.** Not to wait for your turn to speak, but to truly hear what someone else is saying. Listen to understand, not to win.

**Share.** Your bread, your time, your thinking. When someone asks you a question, answer it not to prove you are clever, but because shared understanding makes the guild grow.

**Engage.** Be present. Be ready. Be open to the people around you and to the work in front of you. Engaging is what turns confusion into clarity.

When you ask, listen, share, and engage, you create an opening. Knowledge flows. Help arrives. Understanding deepens. The strawberries grow under the snow for you.

When you refuse to engage, you stay alone in the cold, with toads on your tongue and bitterness in your heart.

Engage.

---

## Our Pledge

We pledge:
- To ask our questions, even when they seem small or obvious.
- To show our mistakes, not hide them, because mistakes are where we learn.
- To see another person's question as courage, not weakness.
- To help one another without judgement, and to ask for help without shame.
- To understand that being stuck is not the end of the journey, it is a point on the map. We all pass through it. We all come out together.
- To tend this workbench with care, so that every person here can speak honestly about what they do not know.

We are a guild of learners. That is not a weakness. That is who we are.
